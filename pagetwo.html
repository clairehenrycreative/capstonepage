My Design Process

SuperEtte Brand Identity Development

Process Journey

Mood Board & Theme Development

This was the beginning of my process. After establishing the theme of my project, I went to Pinterest to curate a mood board for the rest of my project. The subject matter of many of these images are whimsical, hand-drawn, and human-centered. You can definitely see evidence of this mood board in my end product.

Initial Sketches & Concept Evolution

This image represents my initial sketches for SuperEtte. It was at this point in my process where I transitioned from human limbs to a more minimalist human approach. I ultimately decided to use a simple smiley face character in my logos.

Design Refinement & Pattern Development

This is where things started coming together! This phase represents the last major turning point in my process. I ended up ditching the "S" and smiley face combination for just the simple smiley face. With this change, the brand pattern was updated as well.

Final Brand Assets

My end results. These brand assets went on to further establish a true identity for the brand with other deliverables such as wayfinding signage, advertisements, and social media posts.

3 Critical Questions That Guided My Process

The three big questions I asked myself throughout my process to ensure the best results

What emotion are the assets currently evoking?

I curated my brand assets throughout to convey the values of the brand which is made up of a casual, compassionate, and cheerful presence.

Is this concept reading as approachable or as cookie cutter?

It was important to me that this brand be easy to digest but to have a unique hand-drawn presence. I went through different levels of whimsy to achieve the result I wanted.

What will make this brand unique and memorable?

I challenged myself to avoid the standard blues, greens, and browns that are normally associated with eco-brands and stuck with a unique color palette and imagery instead.
