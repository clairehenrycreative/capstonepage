Initial Research

Initial Research that informed my idea. This project was inspired by two key areas of research. The first stems from the sustainability research I conducted last year in our Design Studio class. The second comes from my professional focus in branding and design, as well as past research in that field. I enjoy developing hypothetical brands. I also enjoy exploring how design can strengthen community. This, combined with my interest in sustainability came together to form the concept for SuperEtte.

Online Sustainable Store Concepts

These three stores offer a similar approach to the product-side of Superette that exist online.

  • The Zero Waste Store

    • The Zero Waste Store is an online store that has a large variety of sustainable products in sectors such as home and hygiene, all the way to tech and jewelry.

  • Earth Hero

    • Similar to The Zero Waste Store, Earth Hero has a wide selection of products that are available online for purchase.

  • ZERO Market

    • Based out of Colorado, the Zero Market offers many health and wellness products.

Sustainable Store Concepts in Kansas City

These three stores are Kansas City-based and also focus on sustainability.

  • Greener Home KC

    • Greener Home KC offers a selection of zero waste home and wellness products.

  • Scraps KC

    • Scraps KC is a sustainable upcycling store focusing on crafts that has a community focus.

  • Suds Refillery

    • Suds Refillery is a plastic-free refill store for cleaning and beauty products.

What I Learned From This Initial Research

After looking at the online and local sustainability market, I realized there are definitely similar sustainability concepts out there. This was a really awesome surprise. I'm definitely making it a point to go visit all the ones in my area. However, there was no concert-hosting art-showing community-focused store! I think due to the unique nature of Superette it has the capacity to be successful even in a market with competition. Hypothetically, I think there would also be a great opportunity for these stores that exist in Kansas City to collaborate. The more sustainability, the better!
